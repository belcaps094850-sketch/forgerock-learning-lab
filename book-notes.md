### Key Ideas

**Variation is everything:**
- No process produces identical outputs â€” variation is a fact of life
- **Common cause** variation: inherent to the process, predictable, plan for it
- **Special cause** variation: external, unpredictable, investigate it
- "The root of all evil" = misidentifying which type of variation you're dealing with
- Statistical Process Control (SPC) lets you distinguish random (stable) from non-random (unstable) patterns

**94/6 rule:**
- Only 6% of problems are human error. 94% are system error.
- The system is management's responsibility â†’ 94% of problems are caused by bad management.

**Quality is about process, not product:**
- "Quality is not so much about improving the product as it is about improving the process"
- Defects are a result of process, not workers (Shewhart's insight)
- Less waste = doing more with less

**Management by Means (MBM):**
- If you don't know *how* you achieved your objective, you don't know if you can do it again
- Focus on the method, not just the outcome

**People matter:**
- Ford saw workers as interchangeable cogs; Taylor saw them as machines to optimize
- Both assumed workers don't want to work â†’ adversarial relationship
- Hawthorne Works treated workers as partners â€” and it worked
- Japanese workers believed they were doing something that mattered
- Piecework incentivizes quantity over quality â€” "man's lowest degradation"
- Organizations need to be places where people can teach themselves

**Cooperation over competition:**
- Even competitors working together benefits everyone (especially customers)
- "Strive to be better than yourself â€” that is true competition"
- Deming's real legacy: seeing the grander scheme and preaching cooperation

**On management:**
- "Our problems are different" is a common disease â€” principles are universal
- To change a system, you need knowledge from *outside* that system
- Eliminate slogans and targets â€” they create adversarial relationships
- Improve constantly and forever

### Historical Arc
